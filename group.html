Collaborative Learning, Tailored for You

Group tutorials at BetaPlus Tutoring are designed to offer a powerful, cost-effective alternative to individual tutoring — without compromising on the quality of teaching. Each session brings together a small group of students working on similar topics or enrolled in the same course, allowing for dynamic discussions, collaborative problem solving, and mutual support.

We intentionally keep our groups small — with a maximum of five students per class — to ensure every student gets the attention they need. Sessions are flexible and responsive to the group’s pace and goals, providing a focused yet interactive environment that’s tailored to your academic needs.

Why Join a Group Tutorial?

  • Affordable pricing — split the cost without lowering the standard of instruction
  • Peer-supported learning — hear different perspectives and learn from others’ questions
  • Tailored content — sessions are built around the specific curriculum, assignments, and difficulties of the group
  • Flexible scheduling — regular weekly sessions or focused revision blocks before exams

Perfect When:

- School classes didn’t cover material in enough depth, or concepts weren’t clearly explained
- You’re preparing for a big exam and want support from others working through the same content
- You're aiming to stay motivated by working with classmates or peers in a structured environment
- You prefer collaborative problem solving and shared discussion over solo study

Whether you’re in high school or university, our group tutorials provide a safe, supportive space to ask questions, clarify concepts, and build confidence — together. All sessions are led by experienced academics and educators who ensure the material is delivered with clarity and depth.

Group Size and Pricing

Group tutorials are limited to a maximum of five students to preserve the personalised feel. Pricing is significantly more affordable than one-on-one tutoring, making it ideal for students seeking consistent support throughout the term.

We also offer the option to form your own group — so you can study with friends or classmates from your class or course. Get in touch to find a group or create your own!
